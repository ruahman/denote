\begin{equation*}
3x + 2 \leq x - 7

* ex1:
- you solve them the same way you solve inequalities
- however if yo multiplie or divided an inequalitie then you need to reverse the direction of the inequalitie
  \begin{align*}
  -x &< -5\\
  x  &> 5
  \end{align*}

  
\begin{align*}
-5(x + 2) + 3 &> 8\\
-5x - 7 &> 8\\
-5x &> 15\\
x &< -3
\end{align*}

* ex2:
-for compound inequalites just do each one seperatly 
$3x - 4 \leq x - 8$ OR $6x + 1 > 10$
\begin{align*}
3x - 4 &\leq x - 8\\
3x &\leq x -4\\
2x &\leq -4\\
x &\leq -2
\end{align*}

\begin{align*}
6x + 1 &> 10\\
6x &> 9\\
x &> \frac{9}{6}\\
x &> \frac{3}{2}
\end{align*}

$(-\infty, -2] \cup (\frac{3}{2}, \infty)$

* ex3:
$-\frac{2}{3}y > -12$ AND $-4y + 2 > 5$

\begin{align*}
y < 12 \cdot -\frac{3}{2}\\
y < 18
\end{align*}

\begin{align*}
-4y^7 &> 3\\
y &< -\frac{3}{4} 
\end{align*}
